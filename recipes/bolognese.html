Bolognese

Description

Bolognese is a rich, slow-cooked meat sauce from Bologna, Italy, traditionally made with ground beef,
onions, garlic, tomatoes, and sometimes milk or wine. It’s typically served with pasta like tagliatelle or
spaghetti and topped with grated cheese.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 jar (about 700g) tomato pasta sauce
  • Salt, pepper, and oil

Instructions

  1. Cook pasta – Boil spaghetti in salted water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Cook meat – In a pan, heat oil and cook onion and garlic until soft. Add ground beef and cook until browned.
  3. Add sauce – Pour in the tomato sauce. Add salt and pepper. Simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  4. Mix – Toss the cooked spaghetti with the sauce or serve the sauce on top.
  5. Serve – Optional: top with grated cheese or fresh herbs.
